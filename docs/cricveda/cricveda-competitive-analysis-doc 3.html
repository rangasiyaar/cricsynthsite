Section 01

Executive Summary

CricVeda operates at the intersection of three existing markets: cricket data & statistics providers, fantasy cricket tools & tip services, and sports analytics API products.

No single competitor does what CricVeda proposes: a free, cross-league T20 analytics API with fantasy-specific intelligence built on open data. Competitors are either:

  • Too expensive (enterprise analytics)
  • Too shallow (basic stat aggregators)
  • Too subjective (opinion-based tip services)
  • Too narrow (single league focus)
  • Not API-accessible (no developer integration)

CricVeda's gap: affordable depth + cross-league breadth + API delivery + fantasy-specific focus.

Section 04 — Category A

Enterprise Cricket Analytics

CricViz Enterprise Threat: Low

Founded 2014 (UK). Clients: ICC, ECB, Cricket Australia, IPL teams, Sky/Star Sports. Pricing: $10K–100K+/year enterprise contracts. Venture-backed.

What They Do Well
  • Deepest cricket analytics in the world
  • Ball-tracking technology (speed, length, line)
  • Predictive models (win probability, expected runs)
  • Trusted by cricket boards and broadcasters
  • Cover all formats (Test, ODI, T20)
  • Proprietary data no one else has
What They Don't Do
  • No consumer/fantasy product
  • No public API for developers
  • No free tier
  • Pricing inaccessible to individuals ($$$$$)
  • No cross-league form scoring for fantasy
  • No dream team / captain pick recommendations
CricVeda Advantage
  • Free access vs enterprise pricing
  • Fantasy-specific output (dream teams, captain picks)
  • API-first — developers can integrate
  • Fantasy context, not just cricket analysis
CricViz Advantage
  • Proprietary ball-tracking data (we can't match)
  • Established brand and trust
  • Funded team with dedicated analysts
  • Live match analysis + years of model refinement
They operate in a completely different market. Unless they launch a consumer fantasy product (unlikely — not their business model), they are not a direct competitor. They are a potential PARTNER if CricVeda scales.
SportRadar Enterprise Threat: Low–Medium

Global sports data company. Cricket API available but expensive ($500–5,000+/month). Focus on data delivery — they give you raw ball-by-ball feeds; you build your own analytics. No fantasy-specific recommendations.

SportRadar sells ingredients. CricVeda sells the cooked meal.
A fantasy platform could buy SportRadar data AND use CricVeda algorithms to turn it into insights. We are complementary, not competing.
Section 05 — Category B

Cricket Statistics Websites

ESPNcricinfo Stats Threat: Medium

World's largest cricket statistics website (Disney/ESPN). 50M+ monthly visitors. Free, ad-supported. No public API — scraping prohibited by ToS. The "Wikipedia of cricket stats."

What They Do Well
  • Most comprehensive cricket stats database ever built
  • StatsGuru query tool (powerful but complex)
  • Every international match ever played
  • Trusted global brand (30+ years)
  • Full format coverage (Test, ODI, T20, domestic)
  • Free access to all data
What They Don't Do
  • No fantasy-specific insights (no dream team, captain picks)
  • No cross-league form scoring — stats shown per league, not synthesized
  • Matchup data buried 6+ clicks deep, no fantasy context overlay
  • No public API — cannot integrate into apps
  • No actionable recommendations ("pick this player because...")
  • No confidence scoring on any insight
ESPNcricinfo is like a LIBRARY. CricVeda is like a TUTOR who reads the library for you and tells you what to DO. They are partially a data source (CricSheet uses ESPNcricinfo IDs).
If ESPNcricinfo launches a dedicated fantasy analytics API, they would be formidable — they have the data and brand. However, ESPN has had 25+ years to do this and hasn't. More likely to partner than build.
Cricbuzz Stats Threat: Medium

Times Internet. 40M+ monthly visitors. India's #1 cricket app by downloads. Best-in-class live scoring. No public API. "Fantasy tips" section = journalist opinion pieces, not systematic analysis.

What They Do Well
  • Best-in-class live scoring experience
  • Clean mobile app (India's #1 cricket app)
  • Match H2H and basic player comparisons
  • Team squads and playing XI (fast updates)
  • Strong Indian market penetration
What They Don't Do
  • Fantasy analytics depth is VERY shallow
  • No cross-league form scoring
  • Basic H2H only — no phase/type breakdown
  • No public API (community endpoints break randomly)
  • No fantasy-specific API endpoints
  • No confidence scoring or reasoning
Cricbuzz = live match EXPERIENCE. CricVeda = pre-match INTELLIGENCE. Different jobs. Different timing. Users check Cricbuzz DURING the match. Users check CricVeda BEFORE building their team. Complementary — for now.
Section 06 — Category C

Cricket Data APIs (Paid)

CricAPI & Roanuz Paid API Threat: Low–Medium

Commercial REST APIs. CricAPI pricing: Free (100 req/day), Pro ($30/mo), Business ($100/mo). Live scores, player profiles, match schedules, historical summaries. Well-documented, reliable uptime.

What They Provide
  • Live scores and match updates
  • Player profiles with metadata
  • Match schedules and fixtures
  • Simple, affordable, well-documented API
  • Playing XI data
What They Don't Provide
  • No ball-by-ball historical data
  • No analytics or insights — pure data delivery
  • No form scoring or matchup analysis
  • No fantasy-specific endpoints
  • No cross-league form synthesis
  • No confidence scoring
CricAPI tells you: "Kohli scored 45 off 32 balls"
CricVeda tells you: "Pick Kohli as captain — form 8.7 + venue avg 52 + strong vs spin + opponent spin-heavy, confidence 0.85"

CricAPI is a potential DATA SOURCE for CricVeda (fixtures, playing XI, live updates) — not a competing product. CricVeda may eventually USE CricAPI as one of its data inputs.
Section 07 — Category D

Open Cricket Data

CricSheet Open Data Threat: None — Our Foundation

cricsheet.org — open-source ball-by-ball cricket data. CC BY 4.0 license (free for commercial use with attribution). Maintained by Stephen Rushe. CricVeda's primary data foundation.

What CricSheet Is
  • Richest free cricket dataset in the world
  • Ball-by-ball granularity across 4,500+ T20 matches
  • Cross-league coverage (consistent player IDs)
  • CC BY 4.0 — legally clean, commercially usable
  • People register with ESPNcricinfo IDs (cross-source enrichment)
  • Growing coverage, community-driven, transparent
What CricSheet Is Not
  • Not a product — a data repository only
  • No API — download files only
  • No player metadata (role, country, style)
  • No upcoming fixtures or playing XI data
  • No consumer-facing interface or analytics
  • Community-maintained — occasional gaps
CricSheet = raw flour. CricVeda = the bakery.
CricVeda takes CricSheet's raw ball-by-ball data and transforms it into cross-league form scores, matchup analysis, venue intelligence, phase-wise breakdowns, fantasy recommendations, and confidence-scored insights.
Dependency risks: (1) CricSheet stops updating → new match data stops. (2) Format changes → parser breaks. Mitigations: Archive all data locally. CC BY 4.0 is irrevocable for already-released data. Parser designed for format variations. Long-term: diversify data inputs. Risk level: LOW (CricSheet stable for years).
Section 08 — Category E

Fantasy Tip Services (Human-Driven)

YouTube Fantasy Channels Tips Threat: Medium

50+ active channels during IPL. 100K–2M+ subscribers. Free (ad revenue) + some paid memberships. 1–3 videos per match day. Cover Dream11, MyTeam11, and other platforms.

What They Do Well
  • Accessible (free, video format)
  • Personality-driven (entertainment + analysis)
  • Cover playing XI updates quickly
  • Human insight (pitch reports, injury whispers)
  • Community engagement (comments, polls)
  • Multiple team options (safe, risky, GL)
What They Don't Do
  • Subjective — no way to evaluate who's actually accurate
  • No systematic methodology or verifiable track record
  • No cross-league data (ignore BBL/T20I form)
  • Not API-accessible — cannot integrate into apps
  • Not personalized — same team for millions of viewers
  • 10–15 min video for what should be 30 seconds of data
YouTube channels and CricVeda are COMPLEMENTARY. A smart user: (1) checks CricVeda for data-driven analysis, (2) watches their favourite channel for qualitative context, (3) combines both. YouTube creators are potential USERS and PROMOTERS — "According to CricVeda, form score 8.7, strong matchup tonight" = more credible content. CricVeda's strategy: PARTNER with top channels, not compete.
Telegram / WhatsApp Fantasy Groups Tips Threat: Low

Thousands of groups, millions of members. Free and paid (₹500–5,000/month) tiers. Screenshot-based team sharing. Quality ranges from genuine to outright scam. Massive in India.

What They Lack
  • No data backing — pure opinion, no track record
  • Same team shared to 10,000+ people (no differentiation in GLs)
  • Paid groups often scam or underperform
  • No matchup, venue, or cross-league analysis
  • Trust issues (fake screenshots, selective reporting)
CricVeda Advantage
  • Transparent, data-backed reasoning for every pick
  • Verifiable predictions (public, timestamped)
  • API enables different teams per user context (not one team for everyone)
  • No scam potential — algorithm is consistent
  • Free vs ₹500–5,000/month for paid groups
These groups thrive on social dynamics, not data quality. Different value proposition. CricVeda users will likely still be in groups but use CricVeda data to make better independent decisions.
Section 09 — Category F

Fantasy Analytics Tools

Fantasy Apps & Websites Fantasy Threat: Low–Medium

Several apps target fantasy cricket analytics: CricTracker, ShowBaazi, and many smaller apps/sites. Typically built by one person during IPL, abandoned between seasons, rebuilt next year.

Common Weaknesses Across All
  • Single league focus (usually IPL only)
  • No cross-league form scoring
  • "AI-powered" with no explanation of methodology
  • No public API for developer integration
  • No batter-vs-bowler matchup depth
  • No confidence scoring or reasoning for picks
  • Poorly maintained between seasons
  • No B2B offering — consumer apps only
  • Cannot handle debutants (no global T20 data)
CricVeda Differentiators
  • Year-round (all T20 leagues, not just IPL)
  • Persistent infrastructure — not seasonal
  • API-first with B2B revenue potential
  • Transparent, auditable methodology
  • Open data foundations
  • Cross-league intelligence and debutant handling
The pattern: built during IPL → abandoned → rebuilt. No persistent data infrastructure, no API product strategy, no sustainable business model. Low barrier to entry but also low quality. Some may become USERS of CricVeda API.
Section 10 — Category G

Fantasy Platforms Themselves

Dream11, MyTeam11, MPL & others Platforms Threat: Low → Opportunity: High

Dream11: 200M+ users, market leader. MyTeam11: 20M+. MPL: multi-game platform. All make money from contest entry fees (rake/commission) — not from user win rates.

What They Provide
  • Match listings and contest creation
  • Player list with credits/pricing
  • Recent fantasy point history (last 3–5 matches)
  • Playing XI updates (close to toss)
  • Payment processing, payouts, leaderboards
What They Don't Provide
  • No cross-league form assessment
  • No batter-vs-bowler matchup data
  • No venue intelligence connected to player selection
  • No dream team recommendations (conflict of interest + regulatory)
  • No data API for third parties
  • Stats limited to THEIR platform's matches only
Fantasy platforms are potential B2B CUSTOMERS.
Dream11 could embed CricVeda insights as a "Smart Picks" or "Data Intelligence" feature within their app, powered by CricVeda API. This is the highest-value B2B opportunity.
Why They Won't Build This Internally
  • Business model conflict — contest fees unaffected by who wins
  • Regulatory risk — recommending teams could be seen as rigging
  • Not their core competency (gaming platform, not analytics company)
  • Cost not tied to their revenue
  • Brand damage if recommendations fail
Partnership Opportunity
  • 50+ fantasy cricket platforms exist
  • None have deep analytics internally
  • CricVeda API = instant "Smart Picks" feature
  • Low integration effort (REST API)
  • Even 10 platforms × $49/month = sustainable

Section 13

Threats & Risks

Threat 1
10–15%
ESPNcricinfo launches fantasy analytics API

They have the data, brand, and 50M+ monthly traffic. But 25+ years without doing this signals organizational inertia. Ad model ≠ API model. Impact: HIGH.

Mitigation: Build fast, establish user base and API ecosystem before they could launch. Algorithm/insights layer remains differentiated even if they launch basic stats API.
Threat 2
15–20%
Dream11 builds internal analytics

Regulatory concerns + not their core competency. More likely to partner than build. Impact: MEDIUM–HIGH.

Mitigation: Position for partnership. Demonstrate API value to fantasy platforms early. Be the intelligence layer they outsource to.
Threat 3
5%
CricSheet stops updating or changes license

Community project with single maintainer risk. CC BY 4.0 is irrevocable for existing data. But new match data would stop flowing. Impact: CRITICAL.

Mitigation: Archive all data locally. Long-term: build own ball-by-ball ingestion from live scorecard sources. Diversify data inputs.
Threat 4
20–30%
Well-funded startup enters same space

Fantasy cricket is growing. Cricket analytics is underserved. Attractive for startups with capital. Impact: HIGH.

Mitigation: Move fast. Build API ecosystem and user base. Open-source parts of the platform. Cross-league coverage is hard to replicate quickly.
Threat 5
30–40%
AI/LLM-powered fantasy tools

ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude can already give fantasy cricket advice. Quality is inconsistent but improving. Users may prefer natural language over API/dashboard. Impact: MEDIUM.

Mitigation: CricVeda's structured data is MORE RELIABLE than LLM opinions. CricVeda can POWER LLM tools — position as data layer FOR AI tools, not against them.
Threat 6
5–10%
YouTube / Telegram creators build tools

Most creators lack technical skills. Those with skills rarely build platforms. Impact: LOW–MEDIUM.

Mitigation: Partner with creators. Provide them data. Make CricVeda the infrastructure BEHIND their content.

Section 15

Strategic Positioning

1
Own the "Intelligence API" Category

Data APIs exist (CricAPI, SportRadar). Stats websites exist (ESPNcricinfo, Cricbuzz). Intelligence API does NOT exist. CricVeda creates and owns this category.

2
Cross-League Is the Moat

Anyone can build IPL-only analytics. Building cross-league form scoring across ALL T20 leagues with consistent player IDs, weighted algorithms, and debutant handling is genuinely hard. This is the moat.

3
API-First, Dashboard Second

Dashboard attracts B2C users. API attracts B2B revenue. B2B revenue sustains the business. API ecosystem creates lock-in.

4
Transparency as Brand

Open methodology. Published accuracy. Confidence scoring. Data source tagging. In a market full of "trust me bro" tips, CricVeda says "here's the math, judge for yourself."

5
Partner, Don't Compete

Don't compete with ESPNcricinfo for stats. Don't compete with YouTube for entertainment. Don't compete with Dream11 for contests. Partner with all of them: ESPNcricinfo IDs power our data. YouTube creators use our data in content. Dream11 integrates our API for smart features. CricSheet provides our foundation.

6
Free Tier Drives Adoption, Paid Tier Drives Revenue

Free: Enough to be useful — form scores, basic matchups. Paid: Dream team, captain picks, differentials, H2H depth. B2B: Full API access with higher limits.

Section 01

Executive Summary

Fantasy cricket is one of the largest skill-based gaming markets globally, yet the vast majority of participants make team selections using incomplete data, gut instinct, and hype-driven narratives.

There is no freely accessible, comprehensive, cross-league T20 analytics platform that provides structured, contextual, actionable intelligence for fantasy decision-making.

This document defines the specific problems CricVeda exists to solve, who experiences them, how severe they are, and what evidence supports their existence.

Section 06

Why This Problem Persists

Reason 01
Ball-by-ball data was not freely available

Until CricSheet matured, ball-by-ball data was locked behind expensive commercial providers (CricViz, SportRadar, Opta). CricSheet now provides ~4,500 T20 matches free. This changed the game. Most people haven't noticed yet.

Reason 02
Cross-league data merging is hard

Same player appears as "V Kohli" in IPL, "Virat Kohli" in T20I, different spellings elsewhere. Player deduplication across leagues is a non-trivial data engineering problem. CricSheet's ESPNcricinfo ID register solves it — but you have to know it exists.

Reason 03
Raw stats ≠ insights

Having ball-by-ball data is step 1. Turning it into cross-league form scores, batter-vs-bowler dominance, venue-adjusted ratings, fantasy recommendations, and confidence-scored predictions requires analytics engineering that most cricket stat sites don't invest in.

Reason 04
Cricket analytics companies target teams, not fans

CricViz, WASP, and similar companies sell to cricket boards and franchises. Their price point ($10K–100K+/year) is inaccessible to fantasy users. No one has built the analytics layer specifically FOR the fantasy user.

Reason 05
Fantasy platforms have no incentive to help users win

Dream11 makes money from contest entry fees. A user winning or losing doesn't affect their revenue. They have no strong incentive to make users BETTER at picking teams. The intelligence layer must come from OUTSIDE the platform ecosystem.

Reason 06
"Good enough" YouTube / Telegram exists

Fantasy tip channels create a perception of accessible insights. But they're opinion-based, unverifiable, one-size-fits-all, with no confidence scoring, no matchup depth, and cannot be integrated into workflows or apps. There's room for a data-first alternative.

Section 07

The Opportunity Cost of Not Solving This

For Users
  • 70–80% of fantasy contest entries are suboptimal due to information gaps
  • A user playing ₹50/day across IPL season (74 matches = ₹3,700 invested): average picks yield -₹500 to -₹1,000 loss; data-optimized picks → breakeven to +₹500
  • Delta: ₹1,000–1,500 per season per user
  • Across 200M fantasy users in India: billions of rupees left on the table
  • Beyond money: enjoyment, learning, and skill development diminished
For Fantasy Platforms
  • User churn: 40–60% churn after first season when consistently losing
  • Engagement drops as users feel "outmatched"
  • All platforms look identical — same player list, same credits, same basic stats
  • Lower engagement = lower GMV = lower commission revenue
Differentiation problem with no internal solution.
For the Ecosystem
  • Professional-grade analytics locked behind enterprise paywalls
  • Open data (CricSheet) massively underutilized — downloaded by researchers, not product builders
  • No API product serves cricket intelligence to developers and platforms
Market gap: between "free basic stats" and "$100K enterprise analytics" = NOTHING. CricVeda fills this gap.
Section 08

Constraints That Shape Our Solution

Constraint 01
Zero Budget
  • No paid data subscriptions
  • No paid hosting initially
  • Free-tier cloud services only
  • Revenue must precede expenses
Impact: Use open data, pre-compute, optimize ruthlessly
Constraint 02
Solo / Small Team
  • No dedicated data entry team
  • Manual processes must be sustainable
  • Automation over manual wherever possible
  • Match-day operations must be <5 min
Impact: Smart defaults, confidence scoring, community contribution later
Constraint 03
Data Open, Algorithms Are the Moat
  • CricSheet data available to anyone
  • Competitive advantage is NOT data access
  • Advantage IS the intelligence layer
  • Form algorithm, matchup scoring, cross-league weighting, confidence framework
Impact: Build algorithms first, not data walls
Constraint 04
Legal Compliance
  • No scraping of ToS-protected sites
  • Attribution for CC BY 4.0 (CricSheet)
  • No reproduction of copyrighted content
  • Own analysis and insights only
Impact: Clean data sources only, manual enrichment where needed
Constraint 05
Year-Round Multi-League Coverage
  • T20 runs year-round across 15+ leagues
  • Cannot manually prepare per league
  • Pipeline must be league-agnostic
  • New leagues addable without code changes
Impact: Generic parser + league configuration, not league-specific logic
